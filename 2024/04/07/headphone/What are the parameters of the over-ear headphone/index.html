What are the parameters of the over-ear headphone

2024-04-07
headphone
2.3k words
  • over-ear headphone

Headphones, particularly over-ear models, have several key specifications that can influence their performance and user experience.

Here are some of the main parameters to consider:

  1. Driver Size: This refers to the diameter of the speaker drivers in the headphones, measured in millimeters (mm). Larger drivers often produce better bass response and a wider frequency range.

  2. Sensitivity: Sensitivity is a measure of how effectively the headphones convert electrical signals into sound and is typically expressed in decibels (dB) at 1 milliwatt (mW) of power. Higher sensitivity headphones are generally easier to drive and can achieve louder volumes with less power.

  3. Frequency Response: This indicates the range of frequencies that the headphones can reproduce, from the lowest bass to the highest treble. It is usually given in Hertz (Hz), with a typical range being 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz.

  4. Impedance: Impedance, measured in ohms (Ω), is the opposition that the headphones present to the alternating current of the audio signal. Higher impedance headphones may require more powerful amplifiers to drive them effectively.

  5. Wireless Connectivity: For wireless headphones, specifications may include the Bluetooth version (e.g., Bluetooth 5.0) and supported profiles (e.g., A2DP for audio streaming, HFP for hands-free use).

  6. Range: This refers to the maximum distance from the wireless source (like a smartphone or computer) that the headphones can maintain a stable connection, usually measured in meters (m).

  7. Battery Life: For wireless models, battery life is a critical specification, indicating how long the headphones can play audio on a single charge. It’s often provided for both normal use and with features like active noise cancellation (ANC) turned on.

  8. Charging Time: This is the time it takes for the headphones to fully recharge their batteries, and some models may support fast-charging capabilities.

  9. Noise Cancellation: Active noise cancellation (ANC) is a feature in some headphones that uses microphones and signal processing to reduce or eliminate ambient noise, providing a more immersive listening experience.

  10. Microphone Quality: If the headphones include a built-in microphone, its quality and noise-canceling capabilities are important, especially for phone calls and voice commands.

  11. Comfort and Design: While not strictly a technical specification, the comfort of the ear cups, the adjustability of the headband, and the overall design of the headphones can significantly impact the listening experience.

  12. Additional Features: Some headphones may offer extra features like touch controls, app compatibility for customization, or compatibility with virtual assistants like Siri or Google Assistant.
